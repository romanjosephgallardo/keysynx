## Getting Started

### What is KeySynx?
KeySynx is a crowdsourced music database where contributors share and validate song metadata:
- *Musical Key*: The harmonic center of a song (e.g., C Major, F# Minor)
- *BPM*: Beats per minute (tempo)
- *Song Structure*: Metadata for intro, verse, chorus, bridge, and outro — each with its own key and BPM if they differ
- *Camelot Notation*: A simplified wheel-based key system used by many DJs

### Why Community-Driven?
Analyzing a song's key and BPM requires trained musical ear. Rather than trusting one source, KeySynx lets the community validate submissions through upvotes, downvotes, and reputation-weighted trust — so accuracy improves as more people use it.

### Key Features at a Glance
- *Song Database*: Browse 400+ verified songs with full metadata
- *Advanced Search*: Filter by title, artist, BPM range, musical key, or compatibility
- *Interactive Harmonic Wheel*: Visualize key compatibility and harmonic transitions
- *Community Voting*: Upvote correct analyses, downvote incorrect ones
- *Submit New Songs*: Contribute your own analyses and earn reputation
- *Comments & Feedback*: Discuss songs and help refine analyses
- *Reputation Tiers*: Unlock moderation privileges as your reputation grows

## The Harmonic Wheel

### What is the Camelot Wheel?
The Camelot Wheel is a simplified 12-point notation system that helps DJs and producers identify which keys are harmonically compatible. Unlike traditional key notation (C Major, D Major), it uses numbers (1A–12B) for quick visual reference.

### Accessing the Wheel
1. Click *Harmonic Wheel* in the top navigation
2. You'll see an interactive 12-point wheel with all key positions

### How to Use It
1. *Select a Starting Key*: Click on any position on the wheel (e.g., 5B = G Major)
2. *View Compatibility*:
   - *Same position* (5B → 5B) = same key, perfect mix
   - *Adjacent positions* (5B → 6B or 4B) = one semitone away, good mixing
   - *Opposite position* (5B → 5A) = relative minor/major, seamless transitions
3. *Find Compatible Songs*: Songs at compatible positions play together harmonically

### Camelot to Standard Key Mapping
| Camelot | Major Key  | Camelot | Minor Key  |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| 1A      | A Major   | 1B      | F# Minor  |
| 2A      | E Major   | 2B      | C# Minor  |
| 3A      | B Major   | 3B      | G# Minor  |
| 4A      | F# Major  | 4B      | D# Minor  |
| 5A      | C# Major  | 5B      | A# Minor  |
| 6A      | G# Major  | 6B      | E# Minor  |
| 7A      | D# Major  | 7B      | B Minor   |
| 8A      | A# Major  | 8B      | F# Minor  |
| 9A      | F Major   | 9B      | D Minor   |
| 10A     | C Major   | 10B     | A Minor   |
| 11A     | G Major   | 11B     | E Minor   |
| 12A     | D Major   | 12B     | B Minor   |

## Submitting Songs

### Before You Submit
- Have the song's:
  - Title and artist name
  - Musical key (standard notation, e.g., "C Major" or "F# Minor")
  - BPM (beats per minute)
  - Section breakdown (intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro) if keys/BPM change
- Ensure the song isn't already in the database (search first!)
- Be confident in your analysis — incorrect submissions may be rejected

### How to Submit
1. Click *+ Add Song* or navigate to *Submit*
2. Fill in the form:
   - *Title*: Song name
   - *Artist*: Artist name
   - *Key*: Musical key (e.g., "G Major", "Dm", "F# Minor")
   - *BPM*: Beats per minute (numeric only)
   - *Has Variations?*: Toggle if key/BPM changes across sections
3. If *Has Variations* is enabled, add sections:
   - Select section type (intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro)
   - Enter key and BPM for that section
   - Click *Add Section* to add more
4. Click *Submit*

### After Submission
- Your song enters *Pending* status
- Moderators review it within 48 hours
- Once approved, it becomes *Verified* and appears in the main database
- *You earn 10 reputation points* for each accepted submission

### Rejection
If your submission is rejected:
- You'll see a rejection notice on your profile
- Check for common issues:
  - Key or BPM doesn't match the song
  - Duplicate submission (already in database)
  - Insufficient or incorrect section data
- You can try submitting a corrected version

## Voting & Validation

### Why Vote?
Voting helps the community identify the most accurate analyses. Upvotes boost a song's credibility, while downvotes flag potential errors.

### Upvoting
1. Click *▲* above the vote count on a song
2. You've upvoted — your vote is recorded

### Downvoting
1. Click *▼* below the vote count on a song
2. You've downvoted

### Vote Status
- Your own vote is highlighted (usually in a different color)
- Hovering over vote counts shows a tooltip with total upvotes and downvotes
- Vote counts directly affect a song's confidence score and visibility

### Confidence Scoring
Each song has an implicit confidence score based on:
- *Vote ratio*: More upvotes = higher confidence
- *Contributor reputation*: Votes by high-reputation users count more
- *Validation status*: Moderator-approved songs have higher baseline confidence

## Reputation & Tiers

### Earning Reputation
Reputation points are earned through quality contributions:

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Submit approved song | +10 |
| Receive upvote on your submission | +1 per upvote |
| Helpful comment | +2 (moderator-awarded) |
| Become verified contributor | +5 |

### Reputation Tiers
As your reputation grows, you unlock new privileges:

| Tier | Points | Privileges |
|------|--------|-----------|
| *Newcomer* | 0–24 | Browse, vote, comment |
| *Contributor* | 25–49 | Submit songs |
| *Trusted* | 50–99 | Submit + faster approval |
| *Moderator* | 100+ | Moderate submissions, manage entries, flag errors |
| *Admin* | Special role | Full access (granted by other admins) |

### Viewing Your Reputation
1. Click your username (top-right)
2. Go to *Profile*
3. See your total reputation points and current tier
4. View your submission history and earned points

### Losing Reputation
Currently, reputation points don't decrease, but:
- Repeated rejected submissions may result in moderation action
- Community downvotes don't directly reduce reputation (yet)

## Admin & Moderation Panel

### Who Can Access?
- Users with *100+ reputation points* (Moderator tier)
- *Admin* users (full access)

### Accessing the Panel
1. Click *Admin Panel* in the top navigation (only visible if you qualify)
2. You'll see three main tabs: *Songs*, *Artists*, and (if admin) *Users & Roles*

### Songs Tab (Moderation)
1. *Search and Filter*:
   - Search by title or artist
   - Filter by status: Pending, Verified, Rejected
2. *Review Submissions*:
   - View pending song data (key, BPM, sections)
   - See submission count and vote information
3. *Take Action*:
   - *Approve*: Mark as verified (moves to main database)
   - *Reject*: Decline the submission (user can resubmit)
   - *Delete*: Permanently remove a song (use carefully!)

### Artists Tab (Artwork Management)
1. *View All Artists*: See artist names and current artwork
2. *Upload/Replace Artwork*:
   - Click in the upload field for an artist
   - Select an image (PNG, JPEG, or WebP)

### Users & Roles Tab (Admin Only)
1. *Search Users*: Find by username or email
2. *View User Stats*:

   - Current role (User or Admin)
3. *Manage Roles*:
   - Click *Promote to Admin* to grant full privileges
   - Click *Demote to User* to remove admin status
   - Use sparingly; admins can manage all content and users

- Control how many results appear per page (25, 50, 100)
- Use navigation arrows to move between pages

## FAQ

### General Questions

*Q: What's the difference between a musical key and Camelot notation?*
A: Musical key uses standard notation (C Major, F# Minor), while Camelot notation simplifies it to numbers (1A–12B) for quick visual reference. Both are useful; Camelot is popular with DJs, while musicians often prefer standard notation.

*Q: Can I submit unreleased or remix versions of songs?*
A: You can submit remixes if they have significantly different key/BPM from the original. Unreleased songs can be submitted if you're confident in the metadata. Include a note in comments if needed.

*Q: How long does moderation take?*
A: Typically 24–48 hours. During peak periods, it may take longer. You'll receive an email notification once your submission is reviewed.

*Q: Can I edit a submitted song after it's approved?*
A: Currently, no. If you spot an error, you can leave a comment on the song, and a moderator may update it. Alternatively, submit a correction and ask for the original to be rejected.

### Reputation & Tiers

*Q: How quickly can I become a moderator?*
A: Once you reach 100 reputation points. Submit approved songs and earn upvotes. Typically takes 10–30 quality submissions depending on community feedback.

*Q: Do I lose reputation if my submission is rejected?*
A: No, you don't lose points, but your submission won't earn the +10 bonus. Try again with corrected metadata.

*Q: Can I see other users' reputation?*
A: Yes, on their profile page (if publicly visible). Reputation is a badge of community trust.

### Voting & Comments

*Q: Can I change my vote after voting?*
A: Yes, click the opposite vote button to switch your vote or click your current vote again to remove it.

*Q: Are comments moderated?*
A: Comments are reviewed for spam and inappropriate content. Repeated violations may result in account restrictions.

*Q: Can I see who voted on my submission?*
A: No, votes are anonymous to protect community members' privacy.

### Technical Questions

*Q: What browsers does KeySynx support?*
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions recommended). Mobile browsers are supported.

*Q: Can I download the database?*
A: Currently, no bulk export. You can search and view songs individually. Contact the team if you need data for research or integration.

*Q: Is there an API?*
A: Not yet, but it's under consideration. Stay tuned!

## Troubleshooting

### Login Issues

*Q: "Invalid username or password"*
A: Double-check your username (not email) and password. Passwords are case-sensitive. If you forgot your password, click *Forgot Password?* to reset it.

*Q: "Your account is not verified"*
A: You haven't confirmed your email yet. Check your inbox for a verification code, or use *Resend verification code* to get a new one.

*Q: "Email address not found"*
A: This email isn't registered. Go back and click *Register* to create a new account.

### Submission Issues

*Q: "Song already exists"*
A: This song is already in the database. Search for it to verify or update it. Contact a moderator if you believe there's an error in the existing entry.

*Q: "Invalid BPM or Key format"*
A: BPM must be a number (e.g., 120, not "120 BPM"). Key must be standard notation (e.g., "C Major", "F# Minor", not "C Maj").

*Q: "Sections are missing key or BPM"*
A: If you enable "Has Variations", you must provide key and BPM for each section (intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro).

*Q: My submission was rejected, but I think it's correct*
A: Leave a comment on the submission detailing your evidence (e.g., "Verified with live performance, key changes at 2:30 mark"). A moderator can review and re-approve if warranted.

### Display Issues

*Q: The page looks broken or text is overlapping*
A: Clear your browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Delete on most browsers) and refresh. If the issue persists, try a different browser.

*Q: Songs aren't loading, I see a "could not reach server" message*
A: The API may be temporarily unavailable. Refresh the page. If the issue persists after 10 minutes, contact support.

*Q: Dark mode isn't working*
A: Click the moon/sun icon in the top-right to toggle themes. If stuck in light mode, clear browser cookies and try again.

### Performance Issues

*Q: Searches are slow*
A: Large result sets can take a moment to load. Try being more specific: narrow by BPM range or status.

*Q: Images aren't loading (artist artwork, avatars)*
A: Check your internet connection. If connected, the upload server may be down. Refresh and try again.

### Account & Privacy

*Q: Can I have multiple accounts?*
A: Technically yes, but we ask that you use only one active account. Multiple accounts used to manipulate voting or reputation may result in permanent bans.

*Q: Is my data private?*
A: Your email is private (not shown publicly). Your username, reputation, and submission history are public. Read our Privacy Policy for full details.

*Q: Can I export my data or submissions?*
A: Currently, no. Contact the team if you need historical data or analytics.
